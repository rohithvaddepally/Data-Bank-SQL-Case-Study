# Data Bank SQL Case Study

### Introduction
The Data Bank Case study explores how a digital bank manages customer data and financial transactions using a distributed node-based system. The objective of this case study is to analyse customers who are allocated across different nodes and regions, evaluate customer
transactions, and provide data allocation strategies for different options.

In this case study, I applied complex SQL techniques, window functions, aggregate functions, and time calculations to investigate how customers are allocated to different nodes, calculate balances, and work on allocation challenges. This will help the Data Bank team to optimise their architecture, improve resource planning, and evaluate different strategies for allocating data storage efficiently.

### Case Study Questions

This case study is divided into 4 different parts:
1. Customer nodes exploration.
2. Customer transactions.
3. Data allocation challenge.
4. Extra Challenge.


### Conclusion
This case study explains how to use SQL to analyse a digital banking system’s customer nodes, transactions, and data allocation. The challenging part of this case study is testing out three different hypothesis options and their calculations. Another challenging part is calculating the simple interest and compound interest and aggregating by each month.
Overall, this case study improves the efficiency of the Data Bank’s operations and its planning in data-driven decision-making.
